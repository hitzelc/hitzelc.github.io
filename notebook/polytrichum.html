Polytrichum is a genus of moss. The scientific name is derived from the Ancient Greek polys and thrix - many and hair.

Where I currently live in Merrimack, NH, I see a little species called Polytrichum commune all over the place. This makes sense because it is, well, common.

I feel that I notice them more on slopes (mild slopes even, but rarely on a completely flat surface). I do not know if this is a pattern or if I am just imagining things, I will look into it.

It seems hard to find near me since about late April/early May, seems to have disappeared for the summer? Is this common? Or is it the rainfall? As far as I am aware, we're experiencing a drought right now (June 4th 2020).

7 June 2020: I hiked Mt. Tecumseh and wouldn't you know it: Polytrichum commune (and possibly Polytricum juniperinum).
A number of variables here if we are comparing to the backyard:
  • Lower temperatures
  • Greater inclines
  • Less sunlight
  • More moisture
and of course there are the interactions between these things. The moss seemed to get more deeply green as I ascended.

12 June 2020: I went for a walk along a power line trail behind the community I currently live in. Moss was waiting to be found.
Again, the moss was most abundant in well shaded areas, under cover of young trees or behind stones. Slopes seem to be a must, though I did note a few patches in a dry, flat, well-sunned area between some sloping trails. The moss in this area was small and seemingly young, surrounded by what must have been dead relatives.
